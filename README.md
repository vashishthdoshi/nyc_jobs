# Data Science with Big Data Project: Visualizing the NYC Job Market

## Project Overview
This single-notebook data visualization team project analyzes current job postings from the NYC government, moving beyond simple data counts to tell a visual story about the available opportunities. The primary focus is exploring the "Who?" (Who is hiring, and who are they looking to hire?) and the "How?" (How are jobs and careers structured?) of the NYC job landscape.

## Key Areas of Analysis
* **Hiring Trends and Expectations:** Examining the specific hiring trends and candidate expectations across various city departments.
* **Geographical Clustering:** Investigating the location of job listings to determine if they are concentrated near administrative buildings, business districts, or specific boroughs.
* **Salary Patterns:** Identifying payscales across different types of roles, including computational problem-solving positions versus clerical jobs, and questioning instances where departments pay outside of standard scales.

## Technologies Used
* Python 3
* Pandas
* NumPy
* Scikit-Learn
* Altair

## Future Insights
The analysis specifically looks into misalignments or unexpected patterns, such as highly similar job descriptions attached to dissimilar jobs, or unique hiring locations. Uncovering these anomalies prompts deeper questions and requires the use of other external knowledge sources to provide a more insightful look into the city's talent structure.
